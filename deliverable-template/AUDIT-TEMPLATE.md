## What Makes This Different

Most "landing page audits" are generic checklists:
- ❌ "Add more social proof"
- ❌ "Improve your headline"
- ❌ "Make the CTA more visible"

**PageRoast audits are specific, actionable, and prioritized:**
- ✅ "Your headline 'Welcome to Our Platform' should be 'Get [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe] Without [Pain Point]' — here's the exact copy..."
- ✅ "Expected impact: +15-25% conversion lift"
- ✅ "Priority: Do this first, this week"

## Audit Structure (Non-Negotiable Sections)

### 1. Executive Summary
- Current Grade (A-F with +/-)
- Potential Grade (after fixes)
- 3-sentence summary of biggest opportunities
- Total number of issues identified

### 2. Above-The-Fold Analysis (Most Critical)
For the first screen visitors see:
- **Headline audit:** Is it outcome-focused? Does it pass the "so what?" test?
- **Subheadline audit:** Does it amplify or explain the headline?
- **Hero image/visual:** Does it show the transformation or just the product?
- **Primary CTA:** Is it visible, compelling, specific?
- **Trust signals:** Social proof, guarantees, credentials visible?
- **REWRITE:** Provide actual copy alternatives, not just "make it better"

### 3. Social Proof Audit
- What proof exists? (testimonials, logos, numbers, case studies)
- Is it specific? ("helped 1000s of customers" vs "helped 1,247 customers increase revenue by 34%")
- Is it believable? (too perfect = fake)
- Is it relevant? (B2B SaaS showing consumer testimonials = mismatch)
- **SPECIFIC FIXES:** What to add, where to place it, example copy

### 4. Value Proposition Clarity
- Can a visitor explain what you do in 5 seconds?
- Is the transformation clear? (Before → After)
- Are benefits concrete or vague?
- **REWRITE:** Clearer value prop alternatives

### 5. CTA Optimization
- How many CTAs? Where placed?
- Is the copy generic ("Get Started") or specific ("Start My Free Trial")?
- Is there a sticky CTA on scroll?
- Do CTAs match intent at each section?
- **A/B TEST IDEAS:** 3-5 specific CTA copy variations to test

### 6. Objection Handling
- What are the top 3-5 objections for this product?
- Are they addressed on the page?
- Where should objection-busters be placed?
- **SPECIFIC COPY:** Write the objection-handling copy for them

### 7. Pricing Presentation (if applicable)
- Is pricing visible or hidden?
- Is there value anchoring?
- Is ROI framed clearly?
- Are tiers differentiated well?
- **FIXES:** Specific pricing page improvements

### 8. Mobile Experience
- Does it work on mobile?
- Are tap targets big enough (44px+)?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Is there a mobile-specific CTA?
- **SCREENSHOTS:** Show mobile issues visually

### 9. Technical Quick Wins
- Page speed (check Core Web Vitals)
- Schema markup present?
- Analytics/tracking set up?
- Exit intent popup?
- **SPECIFIC TOOLS:** Recommend exact tools to install

### 10. Priority Implementation Roadmap
**This Week (Do First):**
1. [Highest impact fix]
2. [Second highest]
3. [Third]

**Next 2 Weeks:**
4-7. [Medium impact fixes]

**This Month:**
8-12. [Lower priority but still valuable]

## Quality Checklist (Before Sending)

Every audit MUST have:

- [ ] At least 3 specific headline rewrites
- [ ] At least 2 specific CTA copy alternatives
- [ ] At least 5 "Expected Impact" percentages
- [ ] At least 1 competitor comparison insight
- [ ] At least 3 specific objection-handling copy blocks
- [ ] Mobile screenshot with issues marked
- [ ] Priority roadmap with clear "do this first" guidance
- [ ] Real examples from successful pages in their industry
- [ ] Specific tool recommendations (not just "use a heatmap tool" but "install Hotjar, it's free")

## Tone & Style

**Be direct:**
- ❌ "You might want to consider perhaps adjusting the headline"
- ✅ "Your headline is costing you conversions. Change it to this:"

**Be specific:**
- ❌ "Add more urgency"
- ✅ "Add a countdown timer above the CTA showing 'Offer ends in X:XX:XX' — tools like Deadline Funnel or even a simple JavaScript countdown work"

**Be confident:**
- ❌ "This could potentially maybe increase conversions"
- ✅ "Expected impact: +15-25% increase in signups"

**Be helpful:**
- ❌ Stop at identifying problems
- ✅ Provide the actual solution, copy, or code

## Example Section (Headline Audit)

### ❌ Bad Audit:
"Your headline could be improved. Consider making it more benefit-focused."

### ✅ Good Audit:

**Current Headline:** "The All-in-One Platform for Modern Teams"

**Problems:**
1. Generic — could describe any SaaS product
2. No specific outcome or transformation
3. "Modern teams" is meaningless
4. Fails the "so what?" test

**Recommended Rewrites:**

Option A (Outcome-focused):
> "Ship 2x Faster Without Burning Out Your Team"

Option B (Pain-focused):
> "Stop Losing Deals to Scattered Communication"

Option C (Specific transformation):
> "From Chaos to Clarity: One Place for All Your Team's Work"

**Why These Work:**
- Specific outcomes (2x faster, stop losing deals)
- Clear before/after (chaos → clarity)
- Emotional hooks (burning out, scattered)

**Expected Impact:** +20-30% improvement in scroll depth and time-on-page

**Priority:** HIGH — Do this first. The headline is the first thing visitors see and the #1 determinant of whether they keep reading.

## Deliverable Format

**Option A: Notion Document**
- Clean, professional, easy to share
- Include screenshots inline
- Collapsible sections for detail

**Option B: PDF Report**
- Branded PageRoast template
- Print-friendly
- Feels more "official"

**Option C: Loom Video + Written Summary**
- Walk through the page while talking
- More personal, higher perceived value
- Written summary for reference

**Recommended: Option C for premium feel, Option A for efficiency**
