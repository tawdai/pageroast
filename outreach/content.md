### Standalone Tweets

**Tweet 1:**
```
Your landing page headline is probably wrong.

If it describes what your product DOES instead of what problem it SOLVES, you're losing conversions.

"AI-powered scheduling tool" ❌
"Stop losing clients to no-shows" ✅

The pain → the solution → the CTA. In that order.
```

**Tweet 2:**
```
Hot take: Most SaaS landing pages would convert better with 50% less text.

Your visitors aren't reading.
They're scanning for:
- Does this solve MY problem?
- Can I trust these people?
- What's the next step?

Answer those 3 questions above the fold. Delete the rest.
```

**Tweet 3:**
```
I've audited 50+ SaaS landing pages.

The #1 mistake I see:

Social proof is at the bottom of the page.

By the time someone scrolls there, they've already decided to leave.

Put your best testimonial, customer count, or trust signal within the first scroll.
```

**Tweet 4:**
```
If your CTA says "Get Started" or "Sign Up", you're leaving money on the table.

Better CTAs:
- "Start my free trial" (ownership)
- "Get my first report" (specific outcome)
- "See it in action" (low commitment)

The CTA should tell them what happens next + why they want it.
```

```
I've been doing CRO audits for SaaS and service businesses. $497, 48-hour turnaround, full rewrite included.

Every single page I audit has at least 3 of these problems:

**1. The headline talks about YOU, not THEM**
"We help businesses grow with AI" — nobody cares.
"Stop losing 3 hours/day to manual data entry" — now I'm listening.

**2. Social proof is hidden**
If your best testimonial is below the fold, most people will never see it. Move it up.

**3. The CTA is vague**
"Get Started" means nothing. "Start my free 14-day trial" tells me exactly what happens when I click.

**4. No risk reversal**
If you're not offering a guarantee, you're asking people to trust you with zero proof. Add a money-back guarantee. Put it next to the CTA.

**5. Too many CTAs**
If your page has "Start free trial" AND "Book a demo" AND "Watch video" AND "Read case study"... you're creating decision paralysis. Pick one. Make it obvious.
```
