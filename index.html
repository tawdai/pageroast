The 5 Conversion Killers I See On Almost Every Page

After auditing hundreds of landing pages, the same problems show up over and over. Here's what I'll be looking for on yours:

💀
Your headline talks about YOU, not THEM

"We help companies streamline their workflow..." — Nobody cares what you do. They care what they get. Your headline should hit their pain before it ever mentions your product. If your headline starts with "We" you're already losing.

💀
Your social proof is buried — or fake-looking

Testimonials at the bottom. Logos nobody recognizes. Generic quotes with no photos. If visitors don't see real proof within the first scroll, they assume you're unproven. And they leave. The best proof goes above the fold — not in section 7.

💀
Your CTA is a shrug

"Get Started" is not a call to action. It's a suggestion. A real CTA names a specific outcome, creates urgency, and makes clicking feel like an obvious decision. "Start collecting 5-star reviews in 60 seconds — free for 7 days" is a CTA. "Get Started" is a placeholder you forgot to replace.

💀
There's no reason to act now

Why should someone sign up today instead of next Tuesday? If there's no answer to that question on your page, the answer is "they won't." "I'll think about it" means "I'll forget about it." Your page needs urgency, scarcity, or a time-sensitive incentive — or visitors will defer indefinitely.

💀
Your page makes people work

Multiple CTAs competing for attention. Navigation that invites people to leave. Walls of feature bullets nobody reads. Every extra thing you ask visitors to think about is another off-ramp. The best landing pages have one job and one path. Yours is probably an obstacle course.
